# Research: Why Sites Look "AI-Generated" vs Human-Made

## Common AI-Generated Telltales

### 1. Too Perfect Symmetry
- Every card has the same height and layout
- Perfect grid alignment
- No visual surprises or asymmetry
- **Fix:** Add variety to card sizes, mix layouts, use offsets

### 2. Generic Hero Section
- Big title + subtitle + generic CTA buttons
- Stock image or video embed
- Same structure on every AI site
- **Fix:** Add real elements - announcements, featured news, quick links

### 3. Overly Minimal
- Excessive whitespace
- Sanitized, corporate feel
- No "messy" elements (news, events, announcements)
- **Fix:** Add density - news bars, announcement tickers, quick facts

### 4. Vague Testimonials
- "Trusted by 50+ schools" without names
- No specific case studies
- Generic claims without proof
- **Fix:** Real testimonials with school names, dates, specific outcomes

### 5. Stock-Style Descriptions
- "Making learning fun with AI"
- "Transform your school"
- Generic tech promises
- **Fix:** Specific stories - "St. Paul's College improved DSE scores 15%..."

### 6. No Human Faces
- Icons and emojis instead of real photos
- No pictures of students, teachers, principals
- **Fix:** Real photography of actual people using the product

### 7. Consistent Typography
- One font, same weights everywhere
- Paragraphs all same length
- **Fix:** Mix fonts, vary heading styles, add pull quotes

### 8. Missing "News" Energy
- No upcoming events
- No recent updates
- Static, unchanging feel
- **Fix:** Add News & Events section with dates

## What Real Schools Have

### Hong Kong School Website Patterns

**ESF Schools (esf.edu.hk):**
- News carousel with dates
- Upcoming events calendar
- Student spotlights with photos
- Principal's welcome message with photo

**Diocesan Schools:**
- Announcements bar at top
- Quick links for parents/students
- Photo galleries
- Award certifications displayed prominently

**International Schools:**
- Student work showcases
- Teacher profiles
- Parent testimonials (with names)
- Calendar integration

## Recommended Changes for LSI Portal

### High Impact (Makes It Feel Human)

1. **Add a News/Announcements Section**
   - "Latest: LSI launched at Pui Ching Middle School"
   - "Upcoming: Teacher Training Workshop - May 20"
   - Shows this is a living, active product

2. **Real Testimonials with Specifics**
   - Instead of "Trusted by 50+ schools"
   - "St. Paul's College saw 20% time savings on lesson planning"
   - Include school logo, teacher name, date

3. **Photo Gallery of Real Usage**
   - Students using the platform
   - Teacher training sessions
   - Classroom screenshots (real, not mockups)

4. **Principal/Founder Message**
   - Patrick's photo + personal welcome
   - Story of why LSI started
   - Personal touch, not corporate speak

5. **Specific Case Studies**
   - "How Maryknoll College uses LSI for DSE prep"
   - Real metrics, real outcomes
   - Interview-style content

6. **Calendar/Events Integration**
   - Upcoming workshops
   - Webinar dates
   - Training schedules

### Medium Impact (Breaks AI Patterns)

7. **Mix Card Sizes**
   - Featured tool card larger
   - Others smaller/medium
   - Not uniform grid

8. **Add "Quick Facts" Dense Section**
   - Like Wikipedia infobox
   - Founded: 2015
   - Students served: 9,000+
   - Languages: English, 中文
   - Headquarters: Kwai Chung

9. **Footer with Real Contact**
   - Google Maps embed
   - Office hours
   - Staff directory

10. **Press/Media Section**
    - "As featured in..."
    - News article clippings
    - Blog posts with dates

## Quick Fixes (No Content Creation)

These can be done without new photos/content:

1. Change "Hero" to have a news ticker
2. Add announcement bar at top
3. Make pricing cards different sizes
4. Add "As seen in" logos (even if placeholder)
5. Include "Last updated: May 2026" somewhere
6. Add more specific numbers (not "50+" but "52 schools as of May 2026")
7. Include a timeline of company history
8. Add "Our Team" section (even with placeholders)

---

## The Core Issue

AI-generated sites are **too clean**.

Real sites are messy:
- They have old content next to new
- They have broken links sometimes
- They have announcements that expire
- They have photos that aren't perfectly matching
- They have testimonials from 2022, 2023, 2024 mixed together
- They have sections in different styles
- They have personality quirks

**The fix is to add controlled "messiness" - density, variety, and real content.**
